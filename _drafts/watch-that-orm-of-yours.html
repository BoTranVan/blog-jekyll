ORMs (Object-Relational Mappings) are a way to hide underlying database structure and workings, and provide with programmer-friendly interface to handling database objects. Ideally, a programmer will never have to understand SQL, and will never care about the specific RDBMS implementation she is using.

Nothing is as simple, and ORMs are required to handle complicated cases & understand the different RDBMS flavors. To a programmer, ORMs look cool. As a DBA, one finds drawbacks to using ORMs. Sometimes you just have to understand proper SQL or know your RDBMS if you want to make this work.

I wish to provide with some examples; I'll relate to Hibernate, a popular Java ORM, and Django, a python web framework which includes its own ORM implementation.

- sql obfuscation with weird aliases

- duplicate and triple - indexes
